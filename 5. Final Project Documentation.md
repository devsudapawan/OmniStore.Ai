# 5. OmniStore.Ai Final Project Documentation

## 📘 Executive Summary & Vision
**OmniStore.Ai** is an offline-first POS and business management system specifically targeted at helping shopkeepers in lower-tier markets transition from physical udhar (credit) notebooks into a safe, digital ledger. 

The primary promise of the app is **Reliability over Features**: It must work at lightning speed without an internet connection on low-end Android devices.

### 1. Core Features / MVP Scope
Designed to focus entirely on daily retail reality:
- **Customers & Credit (Udhar / Jama):** Managing trust scores, pending balances, and sending reminders.
- **Inventory Tracking:** Categorizing products, assigning selling/cost prices, and managing stock levels via adjustments and sales.
- **Transactions & POS:** Lightning-fast checkout workflow, supporting split payments, full cash, or full credit sales.
- **Seamless Syncing:** Operating strictly against a local Isar database and syncing implicitly with Supabase in the background. No manual cloud-saves needed.
- **AI Integration (Basic):** Speech-to-text parsing to quickly log a sale or payment, completely replacing manual typing while requiring human review before saving.

*Excluded in MVP:* GST/Tax complexities, Multi-store synchronization, granular user permission roles.

### 2. Project Folder Structure
Built under a **Feature-Driven Riverpod architecture**:
- `lib/app/`: Bootstrap scripts (Isar, Supabase), Routing rules (GoRouter), Global providers.
- `lib/core/`: The Design System. Shared UI styling (Typography, Colors, Fixed Padding Constraints), common error handlers, utility functions (Currency formatting).
- `lib/data/`: `local/isar/collections` holding all generated data schemas alongside Repositories mapping UI actions to Database writes.
- `lib/features/`: Isolated modules representing the app's components (auth, dashboard, pos, inventory, customers, sync, assistant).

### 3. Data Management & Event-Sourcing
The cornerstone of OmniStore is the **Event-Based Data Model**.

- Final states (e.g., total inventory stock or a customer's total due balance) are **never directly stored or overwritten**.
- Instead, the app records an unchangeable ledger of **Events** (e.g., `+10` items purchased, `-2` items sold).
- The "Current Stock" is simply a live summation query of all events tied to a specific product. 
- *Why?* This eliminates data corruption over poor sync conditions, handles multi-device merge conflicts natively, provides an immutable audit trail, and gives total system confidence.
